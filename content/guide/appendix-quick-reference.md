# Quick Reference Appendix

This appendix summarises the four free reference guides on privatepracticeguide.com.au. If you need a quick refresher on any of these topics, the key points are below. For the full detail, follow the links.

## Business Structure

**Full guide:** privatepracticeguide.com.au/guides/business-structure

Most new practitioners start as a sole trader. It's simpler, cheaper to set up, and appropriate when you're testing whether private practice works for you.

| Factor | Sole Trader | Pty Ltd |
|--------|------------|---------|
| Setup cost | ~$0 (free ABN) | $500-$1,500 (ASIC + legal) |
| Annual cost | Minimal | $300+ ASIC fee + higher accounting |
| Tax rate | Personal marginal rate (up to 45%) | 25% company tax |
| Asset protection | None (personal assets at risk) | Limited liability |
| Complexity | Low | Higher (separate tax return, compliance) |
| Good for | Year 1, income under ~$120K net | Higher income, asset protection needed |

> [!tip]
> Talk to your accountant when your net income consistently exceeds $120,000-$150,000. That's usually the point where the tax savings from a company structure outweigh the compliance costs.

**Key steps:** Register an ABN (free, immediate), register a business name if it's different from your personal name ($39/1yr or $92/3yr via ASIC), open a business bank account.

## Medicare CDM Billing

**Full guide:** privatepracticeguide.com.au/guides/cdm-billing

The old GPMP/TCA system was replaced by GPCCMP (GP Chronic Condition Management Plan) in July 2025. Most resources online still reference the old system.

**How it works:**
1. A GP identifies a patient with a chronic condition
2. The GP creates a GPCCMP for the patient
3. The GP refers the patient to allied health
4. The patient gets up to 5 Medicare-subsidised sessions per calendar year
5. The 5-session cap is shared across all allied health providers (not 5 per provider)

**Key numbers:**
- Medicare rebate for individual CDM session: ~$56.70
- Group session rebate (60+ min, 2-12 patients): ~$18.90
- Minimum session length: 20 minutes face-to-face
- Telehealth sessions attract the same rebate as in-person

> [!warning]
> The 5-session cap is shared across ALL allied health providers. If a patient has seen a physio for 3 sessions and then comes to you, they have 2 sessions remaining for the calendar year, not 5. Check with the patient or their GP before assuming you have full allocation.

**Common billing mistakes:** billing without a valid referral, exceeding the 5-session cap, using the wrong item number for your profession, not meeting the 20-minute minimum.

## Provider Numbers

**Full guide:** privatepracticeguide.com.au/guides/provider-number

You need a Medicare provider number to bill CDM services. The number is tied to a physical location, so you need a separate one for each place you practice.

**Steps:**
1. Set up a PRODA account (identity verification with Services Australia)
2. Complete form HW093 through HPOS (Health Professional Online Services)
3. Submit with documentation (proof of registration, practice address, qualifications)
4. Wait for processing (typically 2-6 weeks)

> [!tip]
> Apply at least 4-6 weeks before you plan to start seeing CDM patients. You need proof of your practice address (room rental agreement or lease), so have your location sorted before applying. Home visits and telehealth use your primary location's provider number.

**If you move locations**, update your provider number. This is frequently forgotten and causes billing issues.

## Practice Costs

**Full guide:** privatepracticeguide.com.au/guides/practice-costs

Total startup costs for a solo practice range from $5,000 to $30,000 depending on your profession, location, and setup.

**Mandatory costs (everyone):**

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| AHPRA registration (or equivalent) | $300-$800/yr |
| Professional indemnity insurance | $500-$1,500/yr |
| Public liability insurance | $300-$600/yr |
| Professional association | $300-$700/yr |
| ABN + business name | $0-$92 |
| **Total** | **~$1,500-$3,700** |

**Ongoing monthly costs:**

| Item | Range |
|------|-------|
| Room rental | $800-$2,400 |
| Practice management software | $0-$55 |
| Accounting software | $25-$65 |
| Internet/phone | $80-$150 |
| Consumables | $50-$300 |
| Cleaning | $100-$300 |
| Accountant/bookkeeper | $150-$400 |
| **Total** | **~$1,200-$3,700/month** |

> [!key-takeaway]
> A room-rental setup in an existing clinic is the lowest-risk way to start. You skip fitout costs, share infrastructure, and can change location without major financial commitment. Most new solo practitioners start this way and it's the approach this guide assumes.
